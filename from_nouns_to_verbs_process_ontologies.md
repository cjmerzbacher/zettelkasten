# From nouns to verbs
How process ontologies enhance our understanding of social‐ecological systems understood as complex adaptive systems

Key paradigm: Substance vs. process ontologies

Substance ontologies are:
* Static
* Independent
* “Objects described with nouns”
* See world as comprised of entities independent of one another
* Entities are primary and exist prior to interactions
* Social and ecological as dichotomy
* Equilibrium thinking

Process ontologies are: 
* Complex
* Intertwined
* “Processes described by verbs”
* See world as composed of interdependent processes consitituted with relations with other processes
* Everything is a sequence of events (STORY) [[20200401233013]] practicing_resilience
* Entities unfold in possibility spaces and events reconfigure this space #model

**Social-ecological systems** (SES): complex adaptive systems in which humans and nature are deeply intertwined, endogenous change
