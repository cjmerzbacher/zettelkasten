## Introduction
==emergence==: the way complex systems and patterns arise out of a multiplicity of relatively simple interactions (Obolensky, Complex Adaptive Leadership: Embraching paradox and uncertainty)
* Emphasizes connections over mass (substance)

Humans have traditionally identified with top predators - but these creatures are going extinct due to human impact, while small, collaborative species are adapting.

Organizing in the 20th century has been focused on individualistic, linear organizing: “Many align with the capitalistic belief that constant growth and critical mass is the only way to create change, even if they don’t use that language”. (8) Now we are moving from this hierarchical, patriarchal way of organizing toward a feminine, network-focused methods. [[20200330204247]] as_long_as_grass_grows_giliowhitaker

How can we, future ancestors, align ourselves with the most resilient practices of emergence as a species? Emergence shows us that adaptation and evolution depend on connections and threads, not violent competiton and constant growth. (14) Emergent strategies allow us to practice complexity and grow the future through simple interactions (20). 

“If we can’t articulate more viable futures, and adapt, our human future is pretty hopeless.” (17)

==Organic intellectual== intellectuals outside traditional academic institutions, who develop organically in counter-relationship to the dominant culture. (Antonio Gramsci) #toread

## Principles of Emergent Strategy
1. Small is good, small is all. The large is a reflection of the small.
2. Change is constant. Be like water.
3. There is always enough time for the right work.
4. There is a conversation in the room that only these people at this moment can have. Find it.
5. Never a failure, always a lesson. 
6. Trust the people. If you trust the people, they become trustworthy.
7. Move at the speed of trust.
8. Less prep, more prescence.
9. What you pay attention to grows.

## Fractals
Life is a miracle that can never be recreated. Each day should be lived on purpose. We must leverage not just what we do but *how* we live. “The only way to deal with an unfree world is to become so absolutely free that your very existence is an act of rebellion.” (Albert Camus) #quote

Instead of trying to provide answers to yourself, sit with the questions: 
* Change is coming - what do we need to imagine as we prepare for it?
* What is compelling about surviving climate change?
* How do we prepare not just for suffering, but for sharing and innovation?
* How do we articulate a vision for an economic future that can unite us as the unimaginable happens?
* How do we experience our beauty and humanity in every condition?

## Intentional Adaptation
We respond to change with fear, and are in a constant state of reaction because we aren’t clear about our destination. We anticipate change and stress about what has changed that we didn’t choose. (69)

From Movement Generation: “Instability has become a defining feature of our times...[we can] classify...disruptions...into two groups: shocks and slides. ==shocks== present themselves as acute moments of disruption. ==slides== are incremental by nature...they are not experienced as acute...[we must] harness the shocks and direct the slides.” (77)
 
 From Walida Imarisha: “Nature has taught me about fluid adaptability. About not only weathering storms, but using howling winds to spread seeds wide, torrential rains to nuture roots so they can grow deeper and stronger. Nature has taught me that a storm can be used to clear out branches that are dying, to let go of that which was keeping us from growing in new directions...The question becomes not just how do we survive [storms], but how do we prepare [to] capture the potential, the possibilities inherent in the chaos?” (78)
 
## Interdependence and decentralization
In a hyperindividualistic, capitalist society (@ the United States), every aspect of our survivalis based on our success at being an individual in the world. Healthcare, food and water, childcare, eldercare. Instead, can we build networks of interdependence and decentralized decision making?

#quote “Building community is to the collective as spiritual practice is to the individual.” (88)

Interdependence is a series of small repetitive motions; we are what we repeatedly do. Some examples: 
* Being seen: being soft in your rightness rather than smashing people with your brilliance, can open others up to whatever wisdom you’ve accumulated. 
* Accept your inner multitudes
* Ask for and receive what you need: Interdependence is not about the equality of offers in real time.

## Spells and Practices for Emergent Strategy
“Life is not happening to us. We are learning to be in the actual current moment, to recognize when we have choice...In a terrifying twist, it turns out we always have it. So the great question is; how to be intentional in the present moment, to take responsibility for your state of being, and for your life.” (195) Ask: What if I am responsible for everything?

It is our right and responsibility to write ourselves into our future. All organizing is science fiction. Visionary fiction is a practice of creating the future in our minds, alone and together. (197)

Tananarive Due: “Staring at the ocean, or standing with one’s feet at the shoreline to feel the vastness of the tide as it pulls away...Life and true change are bigger than all of us...we may not all get ‘there’, but we can see it on the horizon. We have to learn to count victories even as we continue to agitate. We cannot become so jaded that we lose joy in everything because the flaws loom too large. The journey is the work, the work is the journey.” #quote
