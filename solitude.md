### Outline
Metaphor of driving at night in the fog. 
1. Begin with the personal: My own solitude, my own driving at night in the fog. 
2. Intro to Arendt
3. Went off to college, discovered productivity gurus speaking about importance of solitude and contemplation
    3. William Deresciewicz
    4. Peter Thiel
    5. Cal Newport (Deep Work)
    6. A Guide to The Good Life (Stoicism)
    7. Part of a larger trend in philosophy since Descartes (according to Arendt): Trend since Descartes has been philosophy as the “exclusive concern with the self, as distinguished from the soul or person or man in general, an attempt to reduce all experiences with the world as well as with other human beings, to experiences between man and himself.” (254)
6. Goal of these technophilosophers is to weaponize solitude and bring it back as an agent of productivity. 
7. They are agreeing with Arendt on the return to the world, but instead of the political realm (action), they wish to return to labor. The desire for solitude is really a desire to escape the need for plurality. Thiel believes freedom and democracy are in conflict because you cannot engineer the wills of people.
    8. On Stoicism: 'Stoicism represents not so much a retreat from action to thinking as an escape from the world into the self which, it is hoped, will be able to sustain itself in sovereign independence of the outside world" (9)
    9. To withdraw from worldly involvement from the trouble and pain it inflicts, into the security of an inward realm in which the self is exposed to nothing but itself.” (310) Arendt
9. Siren song of these people, call of the engineer perfecting the human mind as a machine, society as a factory. 
10. Jenny Odell as one modern intellectual critiquing technooptimization. However, she argues for “standing apart” 
    11. taking the view of the outsider without moving. “Not fleeing your enemy, but knowing your enemy, which turns out not to be the world but the channels through which you encounter it day to day.” (61)
12. This lacks the rejoinder to action, and is sympathetic with the modern malaise Arendt recognizes. Both recognize the context-defining power of solitude, but to Arendt that context is meaningless without a return to other people.
