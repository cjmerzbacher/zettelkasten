## Salmon Boy
“Modernity unbends the attention from cyclical processes into a line. Linear concepts like ‘progress’ or ‘development’ make it difficult to conceptualize the cyclical problem of individual and collective maturity. The cultural knowledge and social structures needed to live deeply inside the land, as one remarkable creature among many, are not won once by a succession of reason-derived deductions and then possessed ever after. Indigeneity as a collective effort, requires periodic renewal, rejuvenation, recomposting” (192)

Power over others vs. power in relation to others. Power need not be a hierarchical concept, it can be a relational one.

Wendell Berry: “What I am against is our slovenly willingness to allow machines and the idea of the machine to prescribe the terms and conditions of the lives of creatures, which we have allowed increasingly for the last two centuries, and are still allowing, at an incalculable cost to other creatures and to ourselves. If we state the problem that way, then we can see the way to correct our error, and so deliver ourselves from our own destructiveness, is to quit using our technological capability as the reverence point and standard of our economic life. We will instead have to measure our economy by the health of the ecosystems and human communities where we do our work.” (203)

## The Salmon Fairytale

Fairytale of Salmon industry assumes that it is possible to build economic practices around the metaphor of world-as-machine and sustain these practices indefinitely. (228)

Instead, can we build practices of machine-as-world?

Gift cycles - Indigenous cultures had technology to drawdown their environment and did not. Problem of collective maturity and tragedy of the commons did not originate with modernity.

“The industry might not be fully ignorant of the larger ecological crisis...but its responses...come consistently from ‘within’ narrative: It advocates more separation, not less, more control, not less; more manipulation, not less. It has no functional conception of the imperative of the return gift.” (240)

“The hardening of reason and ecocide are entagled in a cycle of positive feedback. The hardening of reason contributes to an impoverished sphere of life, which in turn offers fewer opportunities for reason to be brought back into a more reciprocal participation with the body and with the more-than-human world.” (241) 

“Waste is an ontological category that only exists in modern metaphysics. It has no place within a truly living biosphere. Inside a living Earth, there are no externalities.” (242)
