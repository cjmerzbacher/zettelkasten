# Learning to Walk in the Dark
Barbara Brown Taylor

“There is a tendency for us to flee from the wild silence and the wild dark, to pack up our gods and hunker down behind city walls, to turn the gods into idols, to kowtow before them and approach their precincts only in the official robes of office. And when we are in the temples, then who will hear the voice crying in the wilderness? Who will hear the reed shaken by the wind?” #quote Chet Raymo, *The Soul of the Night* #toread

Darkness as shorthand for anything that scares us - either because we do not have the resources to survive it or because we do not want to find out. “Instead, I have learned things in the dark that I could never have learned in the light, things that have saved my life over and over again, so that there is really only one logical conclusion: I need darkness as much as I need light.” (5)

Christian traditions have implied that God exists only in the light and darkness is sinister. This offers people a closet to shove all the things that threaten or frighten them into without thinking deeply about those things. This is ‘full solar spirituality’. 

Spirituality can be rooted in the physical experience of living ordinary days. The days of our lives cannot be easily divided into good and evil, spirit and flesh. We can experience the world as a place of wonder and brokenness. 

“Once, when I stood at the entrance of a very large labyrinth in a very old cathedral, my guide said, ‘From this point on, everything that happens is a metaphor for your life.’” (16) 

## Who’s afraid of the dark?
“To go inside would be like putting down a glass of cool spring water to go drink a store-brand cola. It would be like blowing out a pearl-colored candle to go read by a compact fluorescent light. Why would someone do that? The only reason I can think of is because she does not know what to do with so much night, especially since nothing she can do in it counts as productive, useful, or even moderately aerobic.” (24) 

“Teach her to fear [darkness] by forbidding her to go into it by herself, accepting as collatoral damage the loss of falling stars, glowworms, moonlight on spiderwebs, night owls, cicada songs, and moving chickens in the dark.” (36) Courage is no more than the management of fear. We can practice it by facing darkness.

“Without constant reminders that darkness is not a synonym for moral or spiritual danger, most people I know revert to the equation without even thinking about it...In the full light  of day, darkness becomes the most convenient place for them to store all their shadows: their fear of the unknown, their anxiety about the future, their loathing of their own helplessness, their bottomless dread of death...When the sun goes down...the slower, quieter, and more tactile rhythms of nighttime open doors that remain shut during the day. No doubt there are frightening things behind some of these doors, but there are also stunning things. Eventually, with some practice, **one learns that all these doors open on the same room**” (54)

“If outer darkness is the cloud where we store our inner fears, how much will the real world suffer from our collective fear of the dark? How much will we pay to fuel the engines that keep our world lit, rather than doing what is necessary to feel safer inside ourselves?” (58)

## Hampered by Brilliance

“Sometimes, when I have survived a day of three airports...turning down my dark road has resurrection in it. The deep breath comes first, opening up parts that have not had air all day. The rising feeling follows, as skin prickles with the sudden coolness coming in the windows and muscles clenched against passing trucks begin to relax. Finally an owl flies over the windshield and the soul comes out of hiding, opening in the dark like a night-blooming flower.” (62)

## The Dark Emotions 

“By day I am a servant of the urgent. Nothing important has a chance with me. I am too consumed with the things that must be done to consider whether or not doing them even matters. But in the middle of the night I do not have so much to do.” (75)

#toread Healing through the dark emotions (Miriam Greenspan) “When we cannot tolerate the dark, we try all kinds of artificial lights...there are no dark emotions - just unskillful ways of coping with emotions we cannot bear.” (78) Close relationship between ‘individual heartbreak and the brokenheartedness of the world’ (85)

“One does not become enlightened by imagining figures of light, but by making the darkness conscious,” Carl Jung (86) We do not speak of endarkenment - perhaps because it asks too much. There is no dodging the next cycle of the moon, the next turning of the seasons. 

## Entering the Stone

“I think about how many hours I have spent in therapy instead...walking around the boulders of my childhood...peering down into the holes where I spent months in the dark, wondering why the handholds i can see from the top were invidible at the bottom. The difference between therapy and the cafe is that therapy wants me to look back so I can find another way out, not so I can return the same way I came. Maybe that makes the cave more like a labyrinth. As long as you stay on the path, you cannot get lost - maybe in time, but not in space. The path is circular. The way out is the way in. The path, like the cave, never changes. It is literally set in stone. Only the walker changes, not by looking back, but by moving ahead, trusting the path to teach her what she needs to know.” (126) [[20200330204247]] as_long_as_grass_grows_giliowhitaker

Beginning of story of new life in the dark. A seed in the soil, a baby in the womb, Jesus in the tomb. “No one chooses the dark night; the dark night descends. When it does, the reality that troubles the soul most is the apparent abscence of God. If God is light, then God is gone. There is no soft glowing space of safety in this dark night.” (134)

On middle age, when people know the sacrament of defeat: “With the gravitas that arrives when life is more than half over, people at this stage are ready to spend and to be spent, emptying their pockets in one last-ditch effort to make meaning.” (143)

## The Dark Night of the Soul
 
 “No one asks, ‘What powers do you most rely on? What is the hope that gives meaning to your life?’ Those are questions of faith, not belief. The answers to them are not written down in any book, and they have a way of shifting in the dark.” (144) Peter Rollins says that God is an event: ‘not a fact to be grasped but an incoming to be undergone.” #toread link to ontologies paper
 
 The dark night is God’s best gift to you, as it is about freeing yourself from your ideas, fears, and attachements about God. All of these are substitutes for God. (John of the Cross)
 
 La noche oscura is for healing. “When depression passes, all is restored; when the dark night passes, all is transformed.” (146) Denys Turner: The Darkness of God #toread
 
 “Yes, there were nights of great loss. Yes, the soul suffered from fearful subtraction...And yet what remained when everything else was gone was more real than anything I could have imagined. I was no longer apart from what I sought; I was part of it.” (146)

## Working with Darkness

 “We cannot live in a world that is interpreted for us by others. An interpreted world is not a hope. Part of the terror is to take back our own listening. To use our own voice. To see our own light.” --Hildegard of Bingen #quote
 
 “I have courted the Beloved long enough to know what it is like to receive a divine visit: it is like coming home after a long time away; it is like being held by someone with all the time in the world; it is like remembering a dream that opens a door. Earlier in my life I wanted more specificity than that...[Now] it is enough to step away from time to time, resting in the prescence of the Beloved who accepts all answers, covers all directions, finishes all tasks.” (162)
 
 Prayer from Thomas Merton:
 “My Lord God, I have no idea where I’m going. I do not see the road ahead of me. I cannot know for certain where it will end. Nor do I really know myself, and the fact that I think I am following your will does not mean that I am actually doing so. But I believe that the desire to please you does in fact please you. And I hope I have that desire in all that I am doing. I hope that I will never do anything apart from that desire. And I know that if I do you will lead me by the right road, though I may know nothing about it. Therefore I will trust you always though I may seem to be lost and in the shadow of death. I will not fear, for you are ever with me, and you wil never leave me to face my perils alone.” #quote
