### 3. Bertalanffy’s open systems theory
Transition from reductionism to emergent properties, synergy, and negentropy.
Negentropic: Increasing order (ie. living systems)
Functional and relational criteria rather reductionist analysis of fundamental parts. Organisms exist **in relation to their environment** and are maintained by a continuous flow of energy

Point of reductionism: “Assists in the accomplishment of set purpose, especially in stable environments that barely need to be taken into account, at least in the short term.”(30)
	**There has not been a need for resilience engineering in the Holocene. ** 
 
Primary aim of open system is to ensure survival [[20210112105654]] csholling
Criticism: Structure and function in bounded organizations, boundaries are clear. This encourages people to seek out and identify systems in the world.

Systems view of people: “What distinguishes human beings from other creatures is the creation and use of symbols. (stories)” (33) Symbols: conscious representations of thought and values.

Vertical loading: loading down responsibility to the lowest level in a system at which it can be managed.
