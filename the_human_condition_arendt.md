### The Human condition

The vita activa (active life) has three components: labor, work, and action. Labor is the work needed to sustain the body. Work is the creation of artificial world of cultural artifacts. Action is the activity between people in political life. Action is important to connect to plurality, cycles of natality and mortality. Political bodies create "the condition for remembrance"; action has "the closest connection with the human condition of natality" (8-9)

Christianity ranked contemplation over the vita activa. The distinction of solitude is real, but only has meaning in the context of others. "I doubt not the validity of the experience underlying the distinction \[between contemplation and political action\] but rather the hierarchical order inherent in it from its inception." (17)

"The task and potential greatness of mortals lie in their ability to produce things - works and deeds and words - which would deserve to be, and at least to a degree, are at home in everlastingness." (19). Difference between immortal (Greek pantheon) and eternal (Christian) beings. Sense of christian eternity has made "any striving for an earthly immortality futile and unnecessary" (21)

### The Public and the Private Realm

"No human life, not even the life of the hermit in nature's wilderness, is possible without a world which directly or indirectly testifies to the presence of other human beings." (22) #solitude

In modernity, we have the substitution of the social for the political. "The political is personal." (23)

People are deprived "not of the faculty of speech, but of a way of life in which speech and only speech made sense and where the central concern of all citizens was to talk to each other." (27)

"The medeival concept of the "common good", far from indicating the existence of a political realm, recognizes only that private individuals have interests in common...that curiously hybrid realm where private interests assume public significance that we call "society"" (35)

Sense of privacy originally as dePRIVation of public life. Modern individualism of private life has removed individuality reserved for public realm (41).

Society assumes all members must act as one family, which leads to civility politics as a method of removing the political (39-40)

"If labor leaves no permanent trace, thinking leaves nothing tangible at all...Whenever the intellectual owrker wishes to manifest his thoughts, he must acquire manual skills like any other worker." (90) Thought work

Obsession with solitude in tech comes from an idea of private life driving productivity, not a return to public plurality. Twisted version of Arendt, because instead of acting with others we return to the social sphere to act individually.

Individualization of agents/process is human. Movement of system boundary.

Contradiction in Marx: Labor as both center of world and goal to abolish. “We are left with the rather distressing alternative between productive slavery and unproductive freedom.” (105)

“The Old Testament, which, unlike classical antiquity, held life to be sacred and therefore neither death nor labor to be an evil (and least of all an argument against life), shows in the stories of the patriarchs how unconcerned about death their lives were, how they needed neither an individual, earthly immortality, nor an assurance of the eternity of their souls, how death came to them in the familiar shape of night and quiet and eternal rest ‘in a good old age and full of years’” #solace (107)

### Action

Human plurality is condition for political action and speach. Men must be able to express distinction. People can get others to labor for them or not engage with culture but cannot avoid action.

Arendt argues that action is greater than ‘mere productive activity (180)’. Anonymity and solitude preclude action.

“Nobody is the author or producer of his own life story. The stories, the results of actions and speech, reveal an agent, but this agent is not the author or producer. Nobody is its author.” (184) History as the story with no author.

The end of Trumpism: how will we conceptually see him as politically isolated? 
Action as boundless and unpredictable. “Action reveals itself fully only to the storyteller, that is, to the backward glance of the historian.” (192)

“IT is as though they had said that if men only renounce their capacity for action, with its futility, boundlessness, and uncertainty of outcome, there could be a remedy for the frailty of human affairs.” (195) 

“Both are...unpolitical, and will incline to denounce action and speech as idleness, idle busybodyness and empty talk, and generally will judge public activities in terms of tehir usefulness to supposedly higher ends - to make the world easier and more beautiful or life easier and longer.” Some stem ppl (208)

“The very fact that natural sciences have become exclusively sciences of process and, in their last stage, sciences of potentially irreversible, irremidiable ‘processes of no return’.” (231) 

“Forgiving, in other words, is the only reaction which does not merely re-act but acts anew and unexpectedly, unconditioned by the act which provoked it and therefore freeing from its consequences both the one who forgives and the one who is forgiven.” (241)
