- Is the ES concept (from the late 90s) useful for research? Multiple ways of thinking of it
    - life support services
    - natural capital
    - provisioning/cultural services

- attractive: strengthens conservationist case in era of market-based thinking
- adds variables relevant to humans into ecology
    - ex. pollination, pest control, storm protection

Issues
1. Internal ecosystem processes are not synonymous with services
    2. Extreme functionalism “everything has a purpose” conflates biodiversity with sustaining human well-being
3. Omissions of disservices, tradeoffs, and abiotic resources, coproduction
4. Limits of economic valuation
    
Underlying problem is framed as technological (To Save Everything, Click Here) or politico-cultural
Capitalist structures foster cultures of consumption and selfishness and create myths about technology.
