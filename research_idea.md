### Outline: Resilience in Engineered Systems
#### Introduction
- Motivation: Engineered systems are often designed for maximum efficiency in a small range of operating conditions. Climate change and other extreme swings in environment can easily disrupt these systems, leading to emergent and propagating failures. 
    - Example: CalTrain system, power grid failures, pandemic spread
- We can learn from natural systems (ecosystems, fires, molecular systems) that are extremely resilient.
    - Alternatively, we may want to reduce the resilience of certain systems (poverty traps, viral evolution)
- There are two major ways to view resilience. One is inherent to the structure of the system (networks) and the other is epistemic - that is, defined by our knowledge about a state variable we wish to influence.
- We will use these two different methods to assess the resilience of various biological systems and apply lessons learned to engineered systems.
