# Earth Stalked By Man
Lecture at Barnard College by Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing

"My anthropocene then: Earth stalked by Man. An analysis of man cannot follow him obliviously into universals. Instead I imagine an Anthropocene broken into uneven and unequal patches. Consider it this way: Anthropocene matters because livability is threatened by the repercussions of human activities. We experience livability only through places. Anthropocene is enacted in places even as it is in global circulation. Thus, I begin to wander on a broken path informed by feminist theory."

Anthropocene as both global and local, perspectival, and performative.

"Anthropocene is patch because it is composed of varied assemblages of livability. It exists only in, and through, these patches."

Plantation as machine of replication, transformation of natural things into commodities via removing them from their lifeworlds. This process known as alienation, which creates the possibilities of machines of replecations, simplifies ecologies and allows for the future model known as progress. But also creates the environmental dilemmas of the Anthropocene.

Plantations cannot be everywhere because they depend on entangled interspecies interactions outside of replicability (scalability)

Muddles: No synthesis but instead interruption, time to sit and consider the bifurcation. Stop the world in its tracks.

*Example*: Danish pig farms and antibiotic resistance

Colonialism shows man as an eruption.  The more pure the rationality of man, the stronger the hidden force (magic and madness of colonial interaction)

*Example:* Batavia and Dutch colonies, Fordlandia (Imperial debris)

 ==Ursula K. LeGuin "The Carrier Bag Theory of Fiction"==: Heroism as Botulism: "Livability in the Anthropocene is threatened by just those heroic stories and practices that are thought to have made Man great. Are there alternatives to heroism? Le Guin's essay suggests carrier bags...Collecting offers stories with more complex arcs of temporality, instead of a hero single-handedly making the future, there are entanglements and losses of many kinds."

Ecofuturists: Engineers that do not want to dismantle, but just to keep building. =="The Master's Tools will never dismantle the Master's house" - Audre Lorde.== #toread "They confuse their committment to the environment with masculine self-making." #critique

Third son trope - mocked for sitting in the ashes, yet adept at making friends with animals and spirits as he sets off for a journey? 

==Leslie Head: Catastrophe and Mourning==  #toread

==Oliver Rackham== #toread on anthropogenic woodlands. Pests not as inevitable destroyer. Most ecologies can recover from casual introductions of pests - instead, the industrialization of the tree industry causes rate and virulence of contemporary spread of pathogens. "Catastrophes are not abnormal. It's the rate of catastrophes that is abnormal." One way to mourn is to increase our curiosity about the human and non-human interchnge that together makes our world.

Arts of Living conference #toread

*Example:* Plutonium district residents with bacteria with mutations due to radiation

Modeling as alienation - chain from genes to organisms entirely autonomous, without encounters or history.

Science studies stands outside science, with no need to use the method itself. No need to become a scientist. Instead, feminist science studies sees science not as a monolith but as a patchy assemblage. When do we speak feminism and when do we let it diffuse throughout our work?

Planet as a whole as a unit of analysis - it is and it isn't.

"My passion here reflects a complaint: Why is it that social and cultural theorists have been so reluctant to recognize environmental catastrophe? It's not just the word Anthropocene that puts theorists off. There's something about the idea of environmental disaster that irks us...I think it's something about Man that gets in the way, disallowing our attention to the radical politics put forward by our biological colleages. It's science: we don't trust it, we don't like it, and we don't want to join, even adding our critical redirections. Science, we think, is a form of Man, and if we turn our back to it, we'll enter a brave new world without his interference. Consider how social and cultural theorists have treated ecology. For many political theorists, ecology is an arm of the state. For those involved in indigenous ontologies, ecology is a kind of scientific naturalist, the trademark of the West, and Man. In each case, there are only contrasts to be made between indigenous and vernacular lifeways on the one hand, and science on another...we need a better way of confronting Man."

Patches as the only hope we have against spreading unlivability.
