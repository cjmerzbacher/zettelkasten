## Strong and Weak Emergence

> "whether a plausible metaphysical distinction can be made between things that are "nothing over and above" what constitutes them and things that are "something over and above" their constituent parts"

Strongly emergent - not deducible from knowledge of lower-level domains

CM: This feels relevant to measurement 

LCU: or framing: we build approaches that entail deduction? But also: contextual dependence?

> "For instance: a whole weighs as much as the sum of the weights of its parts, yet the weight of the whole is not something that its parts share" 

Strong emergence requires downward causation ("causation from the macroscopic level to microscopic level" 

Timothy O'Connor (1994): "strong emergent properties are properties that supervene on properties of the parts of a complex object, that are not shared by any of the objects parts, are distinct from any structural property of the complex, and that have downward causal influence on the behavior of the complex's parts"

> "Strongly emergent when it is systematically determined by low-level facts but nevertheless truths concerning that phenomenon are in principle not deductible from truths in the lower-level domain...nomologically but not logically supervenient on lower-level facts"

> "Ontological reduction, which identifies entities in one domain with those in another, more fundamental one and explanatory reduction: that is, a relation that holds between theories aimed at understanding phenomena of one level of reality in terms of a 'lower' level."

> "The distinguishing feature of emergentism is that it denies explanatory reduction and is committed to an explanatory gap"
