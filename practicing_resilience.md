# Practicing Resilience
 
How long have I been discovering resilience? As long as I have been telling myself stories. As the poet Muriel Rukeyser wrote, “The Universe is made of stories, not of atoms.” While the scientist in me disagrees with her literally, the metaphor speaks truth: the stories we tell others and ourselves are the great forces of creation. 

I have told myself dangerous stories: I am useless, I am worthless, I deserve nothing. But I cannot shape those stories if I do not accept their power. And perhaps the most dangerous story of all, the most powerful, is the siren song of *I am an island*. That story is a myth I love telling myself. If I just prepare enough, if I build a big enough moat around me, I can stand on a hill inside the walls of my own creation and need absolutely no one. 

It is much harder to accept my own interdependence, that I am inextricably linked with the world and unable to divorce myself from it or it from myself. That I must take care of others, with the knowledge that one day I will need people to take care of me. And that I cannot do anything to stop that, except create a world where those networks and people exist.
