### 1. The Principles of Ecological Integration

Ecology is “about observation and measurement more than about nature independent of observation” (23)

Difference between analog and digital is difference between a sign and a symbol. Analog sign: uninterpreted, meaningless data. Digital symbol: assigned classification and meaning. (24)

“But we must never deceive ourselves into believing that they are anything but abstractions which we make for our own use, partial synthesis of partial validity, never covering all the phenomena, but always capable of improvement and modification, preeminently useful because they direct our attention to the menas of discovering connections we should otherwise have missed, and thus enable us to penetrate more deeply in the web of natural causation” Arthur G. Tansley (26)

We can argue that even organisms are abstractions. System boundary principle.

On big data: We do not want data for their own sake, we want them for the more general condition that lies behind the data that we got on a given day. (28)

The **industrial posture** Solving problems by building larger structures and applying more power. “The difficulties with industrial age ambitions are that they invite emergence. Emergence involves catastrophic failures of the system manifested before. (37)

Postindustrial problems are also a matter of emergence. “Any steep gradient becomes an accident waiting to happen, albeit a gradient of information or matter/energy. A cluttered high shelf is an invitation for something to fall.” (37) #quote

**Minimal model** - Occam’s razor, gives predictions for the smallest number of explanatory principles.

**Science looks for surfaces that define things with generality** Processes held inside a stable surface usually reinforce each other. (38)

When looking in ecology, time to exhaust supply #metric is a good measure of past selective pressures. (43) This is true in engineered systems as well ; we are often fighting the last war.

Hierarchies can be nested or non-nested. For example, cells aggregate to form a tissue in a nested hierarchy. Predators eat prey in a non-nested hierarchy - the prey is not contained within the predator.

“The point is not to find which assumptions are right, but what lies you can get away with. You simply cannot get away with the assumption of closure in biology.” (58)

**Recognition of role of observer**: “Ignoring human subjectivity will not make it go away. Since one makes arbitrary decisions anyway, ignoring them abdicates responsibility needlessly.” (59)

Model definition #model”: **GRAIN** and **EXTENT**
Grain is how closely you look at something.
Extent is how big your system is.
