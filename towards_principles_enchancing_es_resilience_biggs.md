1. Maintain diversity and redundancy
2. Manage connectivity
3. Manage slow variables and feedbacks
4. Foster and understanding of SES as complex adaptive systems
5. Encourage learning and experimentation
6. Broaden participation
7. Promote polycentric governance systems

Ensuring reliable flow of ES in response to climate change, urbanization, growing population, rising consumption, and increased global connectivity

Must specify **resilience of what to what**

Diversity and redundancy are important for resilience
    - Response diversity
    - Functional redundancy
    - Heterogeneity of landscapes
- Resilience compromised when	
    - Keystone species removed
    - very high redundancy decreases productivity, stagnation

Connectivity: if too high, disturbances can propagage rapidly

May need to disrupt and weaken or create feedbacks ex. fire suppression has created one regime, but need to burn fires.

Much resilence science is incredibly general or very specific. We need theory to fill in the gap.
