- Frontier AI models are highly capable of bioengineering but result in consistent, frustrating refusals even for routine biological design work
" Even a sophisticated actor untrained in biology would need extended interaction with a model to get from chat transcript to viable agent. Refusal treats every such interaction as the last line of defense. Alignment treats them as a sequence of opportunities."
- Refusal has commercial costs - if software is not usable/useful, not adopted.
- For bad actors, open source models already offer good-enough performance; practical effects of refusal are that good actors are sidelined while bad actors iterate freely.
